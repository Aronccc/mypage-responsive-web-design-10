Alan Turing

The father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence

Here is a timeline of Alan Turing's life:

  • 1912 - Born in Maida Vale, London.
  • 1931 - Enters King's College, Cambridge.
  • 1936 - Publishes "On Computable Numbers," introducing the concept of the Turing Machine.
  • 1939 - Joins the Government Code and Cypher School at Bletchley Park to crack the Enigma code.
  • 1945 - Awarded the OBE (Order of the British Empire) for his wartime services.
  • 1950 - Publishes "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," proposing the famous "Turing Test."
  • 1952 - Convicted of gross indecency due to his sexuality, leading to the loss of security clearance.
  • 1954 - Passes away at the age of 41.
  • 2013 - Granted a posthumous royal pardon by Queen Elizabeth II.
